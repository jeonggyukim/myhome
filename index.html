About

I am a fourth-year graduate student in the Department of Physics and Astronomy at Seoul National University, Republic of Korea. I am working with Prof. Woong-Tae Kim for my PhD degree.

Research

My main research interest is in understanding dynamics of H II regions and their impact on the surrounding interstellar medium. Using linear stability analysis as well as numerical simulations, I have previously studied gravitational instability of a thin layer confined by an external pressure (Kim et al. 2012), interfacial instability of evaporation fronts in the ISM (Kim & Kim 2013), wiggle instability of galactic spiral shocks (Kim et al. 2014), and instability of ionization fronts surrounding H II regions (Kim & Kim 2014).
